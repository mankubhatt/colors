Photopic vision: normal cone-based color perception
Purkinje Effect
At low light, rod cells dominate — they peak at 507 nm (blue-green). Blues and greens appear relatively brighter while reds fade. Named after Jan Purkyně, 1825.
Hunt Effect
Colors appear more vivid and saturated at higher luminance. At very dark or very bright extremes, perceived saturation drops — colors look washed out.
Bezold-Brücke Hue Shift
As luminance rises, hues shift: reds push toward yellow, blues push toward cyan. Two "invariant" hues near cyan (~478 nm) and yellow (~572 nm) stay stable.
Stevens Effect
Perceived contrast follows a power law (brightness ≈ L^0.33). In bright light, the difference between dark and light areas feels much more extreme.
